Margin vs Border vs Padding

Imagine a postcard in a picture frame hanging on a wall.

  • Padding is the mat board inside the frame—the cushion between the postcard and the frame.
  • Border is the frame itself—the visible edge around the postcard.
  • Margin is the empty wall space around the frame—how far it sits from other frames. 
[ margin ]   ← outside space (separates from neighbors)
[  border ]  ← visible edge
[ padding ]  ← inside space (breathing room for the content)
[ content ]  ← the text/image itself

Why it matters

Padding (inside space)

  • Makes text and images easier to read by giving them breathing room.
  • Increases the clickable/tap area of buttons.
  • Background colour shows here because it’s inside the box.

Border (the frame)

  • Adds a clear outline to separate a box from what’s around it.
  • Can be thin, thick, dashed, rounded—purely visual decoration.

Margin (outside space)

  • Controls the gap between boxes.
  • Doesn’t change the clickable area.
  • Always transparent—no colour fills here.

Quick cheat sheet

  • Need your button to feel bigger and easier to tap? Add padding.
  • Need a clearer edge? Add a border.
  • Need more space between sections? Add margin.
  • Background colour fills content + padding, but not the margin.

Remember: Padding = inside comfort, Border = the frame, Margin = outside breathing room.
