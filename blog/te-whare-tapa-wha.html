Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā, developed by Tā Mason Durie, describes wellbeing as a wharenui (meeting house) with four walls—Taha Tinana (physical), Taha Hinengaro (mental and emotional), Taha Whānau (family and social), and Taha Wairua (spiritual)—standing on the foundation of Whenua (connection to land and place). Balance across all dimensions sustains strength; when one wall is weak, the whole house is affected.
Te Whare Tapa Whā — Personal Wellbeing Plan
Wall Activities
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)
  • Go to a gym class twice a week (Mon & Thu).
  • Go for a short bike ride twice a week.
Taha Whānau (Family & Social Wellbeing)
  • Tech-free dinner at the table with my partner, 5 nights/week.
  • Call Mum every Sunday.
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional & Mental Wellbeing)
  • Minimum 5 minute guided meditation before sleep.
  • Study in 50/10 Pomodoros; short walk during breaks.
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)
  • Morning gratitude: list 3 things.
  • 20-minute nature sit-spot (beach or Olive grove) 1–2×/week.
Whenua (Land & Environment)
  • Walk in the Olive grove once a week.
  • Walk on the beach once a week.
