My Learning Plan

My long-term goal is to learn more about programming and develop the skills to build my own projects. I’ve worked in software development for about 15 years, and I would like to have higher-quality conversations with developers.

For this course, my strengths include familiarity with some concepts and terminology that I’ve picked up from working in development teams. My limitations include staying focused and being methodical enough to follow the steps without jumping around.

I would like to practice collaboration with others as a contributor rather than as an outsider or a manager of the output. I also hope to be more patient with people I don’t naturally connect with as much as others.

I commit to studying and coding for at least five hours per day during the week, with any additional catch-up at the weekend to finish the sprint. I will read shared blogs, learn from others, and encourage them. In Sprint 2 I lost more time than I should have with a technical issue; I should have asked for help earlier, as the facilitator was able to get me back on track quickly and share a few extra tips I might not otherwise have picked up. I believe information is retained better when it’s from conversation or collaboration.

I will timebox attempts to resolve blockers and try to resolve them myself using the handbook or ChatGPT. I will seek help from my facilitator if I don’t feel comfortable asking my peers.

I hope that the facilitators will continue to provide one-to-one guidance if requested and remain patient and calming, as I have already found David to be.

I will not be online between 9:15 and 11:00 am most days so that I can attend a gym class and do other life admin, although this is negotiable.
